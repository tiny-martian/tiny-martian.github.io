We live in such an amazing time. Information is ubiquitous and limitless and intoxicating. I am a god: I can sink into flawless virtual reality, stream vast playlists of music, craft seamless vector art. My thumbs are strong as oxen. I can indulge in trendy analog technologies, picking and choosing the most aesthetic vestiges of each generation: writing calligraphy with flex nibs, playing on a grand piano, snapping Polaroids. Any possible version of myself that I contrive is given legitimacy.

I am unconstrained by form.
